When Period Problems Are a Sign to Plan for the Future – PCOS, Fertility & Pregnancy

Introduction

Missed periods. Acne. Hair fall. Tired all the time?

You might brush these off as “normal.” But they could be early signs of PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) — a common but often misunderstood condition that affects hormones, skin, mood, and even future chances of pregnancy.

What is PCOS?

It’s a hormonal imbalance that can:

• Delay or stop periods

• Cause weight gain & facial hair

• Affect fertility & ovulation

• Trigger mood swings and acne

Pain Point: “I didn’t know it was serious until I tried for a baby.”

Many women discover PCOS only when they face difficulty getting pregnant. That’s why early diagnosis matters.

How Kaade Hospital Helps:

Our team of expert Gynaecologists and Endocrinologists work together to:

• Run hormonal tests & scans

• Guide with PCOS-specific diets

• Suggest lifestyle changes

• Help with fertility planning

We don't just treat the symptoms — we treat you.

From PCOS to Parenthood – Yes, it’s Possible!

With the right care and monitoring, you can conceive and enjoy a healthy pregnancy. We walk with you from irregular periods to baby bumps.

Final Word

If your periods are irregular, painful, or unpredictable — don’t wait. The earlier you get help, the better your future health (and motherhood journey) will be.

4.Lower Risk of Infection: Smaller incisions reduce the risk of wound infections and other complications.

Conditions Treated with Laparoscopy at Kaade Hospital

At Kaade Hospital, laparoscopic techniques are used for a wide range of conditions, including:

Gallbladder Surgery: Removal of gallstones or the gallbladder.

Hernia Repair: Strengthening weak areas of the abdominal wall.

Appendectomy: Removal of an inflamed appendix.

Gynecological Procedures: Treatment for ovarian cysts, endometriosis, or hysterectomy.

Bariatric Surgery: Weight loss procedures like gastric bypass or sleeve gastrectomy.

Colorectal Surgery: Treatment for issues like colon cancer or inflammatory bowel disease.

How Does Laparoscopic Surgery Work?

Preparation: The patient is prepped with minimal anesthesia requirements.

Incisions: Small incisions (typically 0.5–1.5 cm) are made to insert the laparoscope and surgical tools.

Procedure: The surgeon views the area on a monitor and performs the procedure with precision.

Closure: Tiny sutures or adhesive strips are used to close the incisions.
